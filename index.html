Purpose

  • people planning sprints this summer, hoping to help get D8 released will know the things that will help them with their goal
  • provoke conversation
  • give some opinions about what makes a successful sprints that other people rarely say out loud
  • collectively learn more things about what makes sprints effective from the conversation part
  • longer term goals
    • contrib
    • 8.1.x

Agreed basics, community consensus is a good idea, non controversial things

Venue

  • dedicated space (not starbucks)
  • electricity: power strips at each table
  • tables that are for groups facing each other
  • movable chairs
  • drinks: water, coffee, tea, soda, if have beer, have craft non-alcoholic drinks
  • natural light
  • large writing board: chalk board, white board, flip chart, giant post-its
  • climate control: air conditioning, windows that open
  • parking

internet (really the most important thing ever)

  • wifi or wired
  • backup wifi
  • freenode
  • tell DA infra (so no false positive DOS indicator)
  • testbots, have someone who can start more. testing delays suck and frustrate sprinters.

food (snacks and lunch)

  • in the venue, or venue which allows bringing food in
  • deconstructed food with ingredients labeled, or vegan, vegetarian, omnivore
  • meals available for other dietary restrictions (gluten free, halal, etc.)
  • close to food (dinner)

summary: convenience and quality matters

Community building

  • continuity of group between sprints
  • make it fun: organize things in physical space. papers. posters. stickers.
  • outings around the sprint -- dinners together, local tours, etc.
  • t-shirts or more lighthearted identifiers (we've had electrical tape, aprons, leis, and even bunny ears recently) for sprint leads and participants
  • chocolate or specialties from the country people are from
  • build some local color into the schedule for the out of town people

The triggers for holding a sprint

  • someone has a burning interest in getting a particular thing done
  • maintainers, release managers aware of gaps
  • existing vague, scary area, not moving forward organically
  • grant or other funding: plane tickets, hotel, venue food

Global State

Set data-state="something" on a slide and "something" will be added as a class to the document element when the slide is open. This lets you apply broader style changes, like switching the page background.

State Events

Additionally custom events can be triggered on a per slide basis by binding to the data-state name.
